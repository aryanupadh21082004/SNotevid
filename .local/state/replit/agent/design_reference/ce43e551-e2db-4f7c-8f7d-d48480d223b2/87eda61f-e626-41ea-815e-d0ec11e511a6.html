1. Understanding Derivatives

A derivative represents the rate of change of a function at any given point. It's fundamental to calculus and has wide applications in physics, economics, and engineering [1].

  • Geometric interpretation: slope of tangent line
  • Physical interpretation: instantaneous velocity
  • Notation: f'(x), dy/dx, or Df
